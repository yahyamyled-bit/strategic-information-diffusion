# Early-detection backfire -- artifact test summary

**Purpose.** Disentangle preemptive-flagging backfire from residual-transmission backfire.

**Configuration.** k=500 degree-targeted hubs, t_detect=10, F absorbing, alpha=0.30, beta=0.80, gamma=0.36, default eta=0.10, random-uniform seeds, MASTER_SEED=20260612, n_runs=50 per cell.

**Sanity check.** Observed CE at eta=0.50 = -65.49% vs main-run reference -65.49% (Delta = +0.0000 pp). The cell condition_id matches the main run; reproduction is exact up to floating-point.

**Baseline spread (this experiment, n=50) = 71,509.1 nodes.**

## Part B -- Would the flagged targets have shared?

On a single instrumented baseline run, of the 500 early-detection target hubs:
- At t = 10: **429 still S** (eligible for flagging), 33 I, 38 R, 0 F.
- Of those still S at t=10: **0 ever enter I** over the rest of the baseline run (0.0%); **429 remain truly silent** (100.00%).

## Verdict

The CE-vs-eta curve is **monotone**: containment goes from +1.49% at eta=0 to -65.49% at eta=0.50. The backfire at eta=0.50 is therefore not a property of preemptive targeting itself -- at eta=0 the same intervention CONTAINS spread. Part B confirms the artifact mechanism: only 0.0% of the targets still S at t=10 ever enter state I in the no-intervention baseline. The remaining 100.0% are truly silent under the model -- flagging them into F with eta=0.50 INJECTS exposure that the model otherwise says would not exist. **Conclusion.** The -65.5% backfire at eta=0.50 is largely a consequence of the assumed residual-transmission factor applied to nodes the model treats as low-probability sharers, not a property of preemptive targeting per se. The early-detection backfire is therefore conditional on the residual-transmission assumption rather than unconditional: the design is fragile to the eta assumption, not unconditionally broken.
